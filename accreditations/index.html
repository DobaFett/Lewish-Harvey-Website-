Accreditations & standards

Anyone can say "fully qualified". Accreditation means someone independent checks. Here's what stands behind our work — and what each badge actually means for you.

NICEIC

The UK's long-established register for electrical contractors. NICEIC assessment covers the competence of our work, our processes and our certification — checked by an independent body, not self-declared.

SafeContractor logo
to be added

SafeContractor

Independent health & safety accreditation. For businesses, schools and care settings, it means our safety practices have been audited — useful evidence for your own compliance and procurement checks.

EV installer credentials
to be added

Authorised EV installers

Authorised installers of home and workplace EV charge points — installations that start with a proper site survey and load check, and end with compliant certification and aftercare.

18th Edition

18th Edition Wiring Regulations

All work is carried out to BS 7671, the current national standard for electrical installations — the benchmark every certificate we issue is measured against.

RAMS

Risk assessments & method statements

Issued before work is undertaken, on every job. Your building manager, head teacher or compliance officer gets the paperwork before a single cable is touched.

Guarantee

Work guaranteed

All work is guaranteed and carried out by time-served, fully qualified technicians — with professional advice before, during and after installation.

What this means for you

Fewer risks, better paperwork, no surprises

Accreditation isn't decoration — it's what lets a school, care provider or facilities manager put us on site without a second thought, and what tells a homeowner the certificate they receive will mean something. If your procurement process needs copies of our accreditation documents, just ask.
