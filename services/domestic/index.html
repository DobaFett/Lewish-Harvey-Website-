Common questions

Domestic FAQs

My fuse board keeps tripping — what should I do?

Frequent tripping is your installation telling you something's wrong — a faulty appliance, damaged cable or overloaded circuit. Unplug what you can and give us a call: fault finding is one of our bread-and-butter jobs.

Is a small job too small to call you about?

No. Extra sockets, a new light fitting, a broken switch — small jobs are part of the service. It's often how customers first find us before trusting us with bigger work.

How do I know if my house needs rewiring?

Age of the installation, old rubber or fabric-covered cables, an outdated fuse board, or sockets and lights behaving oddly are all warning signs. The honest answer comes from an EICR (Electrical Installation Condition Report) — we inspect, test and give you a clear report on what's safe and what needs attention.

Will I get a certificate for the work?

Yes — where the work requires certification you'll receive the proper paperwork, and everything is carried out to the 18th Edition Wiring Regulations. Keep it safe: you'll want it when you sell the house.

Can you fit an EV charger at my house?

Yes — we're authorised home EV charge point installers. We survey your supply and run a load check first, so the charger you get is one your home can actually support. More on EV charging.

I'm a landlord — can you sort my certificates?

Yes. We carry out EICRs and landlord safety certificates, handle any remedial work the report flags, and can set up reminders as part of a maintenance arrangement. More on testing & compliance.
